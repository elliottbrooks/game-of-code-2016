Game of Code 2016

Proposal: Attain

Presented by Elliott Brooks

The Problem

  • Many existing applications attempt to present users with a view of their progress:
    • by tracking metrics like weight
    • by tracking behaviours like exercise
    • by tracking other factors like user nutrition

  • Many of them fail to consider:
    • Goals vary from user to user: in type, in length, in magnitude
    • Users usually wish to persue multiple goals

The Solution?

Create a framework where users can create, monitor, and ATTAIN their own goals.

  • Lose Weight
  • Eat Better
  • Revise More
  • Drink More Water
  • Eat Out Less
  • Spend Less on Clothes
  • Spend More Time with Friends
  • Avoid Sugary Foods
  • Floss More Often
  • Exercise Three Times a Week
  • Get Takeaway Food Less Often
  • Get More Regular Sleep

How it Works

The user chooses a metric and a goal. Not every goal needs to be tied to a metric, and some metrics can be lagging indicators.

A Metric is a variable that is measured that the user has no direct and immediate control over, for example weight or average grade.
A Goal is a statement of intention to modify or maintain a set of behaviours, by affecting things that the user does have direct and immediate control over, such as aiming to drink 7 glasses of water per day, or getting 8 hours of sleep a night.

Measuring Success

To combat the information overload associated with tracking multiple behaviours, we present users with a simple and intuitive measure of their current progress - to the minute.

Users can match Metrics to their Goals

Supported Metrics

Any numeric measurement can be added as a metric and tracked over time. Users can be regularly prompted to enter measurements.

Preset Metrics

  • Body Weight
  • Body Fat Percentage
  • Body Measurement
  • Grade Average

Custom Metrics

  • Numeric metric, progressive values
  • Average metric, average value over time

Supported Goals

Nutritional goals share data, and can be inputted through barcode scanning of food and ingredients.

Preset Goals

  • Food and Nutrition (Calories, Macros)
  • Water Consumption
  • Sleep Duration
  • Activity and Exercise

Custom Goals

  • Frequency Goal (Do something x times a week)
  • Time Management Goal (Spend more/less time a day doing something)
  • Financial Goal

Prototype Deliverable

  • An Android application capable of
    • Creating a user's goals and metrics
    • Tracking progress against them
    • An emphasis on nutritional goals
    • Aggregating goal progress into a single measure
    • Some graphing output

Future implementation ideas

  • Server-side application, capable of
    • Saving user progress and goals across devices
    • Serving content to the application
    • Suggesting products for users
  • A companion application for iOS
  • A web interface
  • Social features
